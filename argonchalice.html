Characters

Lucerna

Lucerna, the protagonist, possesses the blood of two powerful Gods. She is unaware of this, as the only thing she remembers is being raised by Ericus as an infant. He inducted Lucerna into the Dawnbreakers at a young age due to displaying a natural affinity for magic. Despite this, she is shunned by other guild members, claiming that she is “tainted by darkness.”

Ericus the Saviour

Ericus the Saviour cast out the Gods from Eisos during The Convergence with powerful magic. He is now dying from a disease that he inherited long ago.

The Tainted

The blood that rained down over Eisos during The Crimson Years granted humans great powers. Despite this, some of them reacted negatively to it, turning into dark entities known as The Tainted. They want nothing but flesh and power.

The Dawnbreakers

The Dawnbreakers were formed under the guidance of Ericus. They have maintained the magical shield encompassing Eisos for centuries, keeping the Gods from ever entering it. This peace is known as the Equilibrium.

Mendax & Lumenia

Mendax is the Umbran God of Chaos. He eloped with Lumenia, the Luxian Goddess of Life, to the realm of Eisos. This caused the two clans of Gods, the Umbrans and Luxians, to wage war against each other. Throughout the game, Ericus reveals to Lucerna that these Gods are her parents.

Problem

The original Argon Chalice game is a 3D platformer. The team identified that no storyline was present, which made us uninterested in achieving the game's goal: to play through all of the levels. Very basic mechanics were also present in the original game, such as jumping, avoiding obstacles, and reaching higher platforms to finish a level. Thus, the team wanted to incorporate a narrative that encouraged level completion, as well as mechanics that kept the player constantly engaged in gameplay.

Solution

The team decided to redesign the entire game from 3D to 2D, as it was more efficient to create art for the latter style. This allowed the team to focus on implementing a dialogue system which allowed characters to communicate back and forth with each other during gameplay, revealing to the player key plot points of the story. Tower-defense mechanics were also implemented, as it matched the fact that the protagonist, Lucerna, was able to summon magical golems for offensive and defensive purposes.

Mechanics

Essence Management

Lucerna can shoot spells and summon golems by spending Essence. Essence can be replenished by killing enemies.

Summon Mode

Lucerna can enter Summon Mode, which enables her to summon golems on the field (indicated by highligted squares known as nodes) that serve different purposes in combat.

Upgrades

When in Summon Mode, Lucerna is also able to upgrade golems by spending Essence. This boosts their stats such as their damage, rate of fire, and range.

Abilities

RIGHT-CLICK

Staff of Duality

Lucerna's staff enables her to cast powerful spells for 25 Essence. Each kill replenishes 50 Essence.

HIGH DPS

Lightning Golem

Lucerna summons a Lightning Golem that shoots spells rapidly. Costs 200 Essence to summon.

SLOWS

Ice Golem

Lucerna summons an Ice Golem that shoots a slowing beam at enemies. Costs 300 Essence to summon.

AOE DAMAGE

Fire Golem

Lucerna summons a Fire Golem that deals damage to multiple enemies in a small radius. Costs 400 Essence to summon.

Gameplay

The player must defend the Chalice of Everlasting Life from waves of The Tainted. Once they reach the final level, they will be given a choice: Bring back the chalice to cure Ericus, or Drink from the chalice to gain power. Each choice incites a different boss fight. Beating each wave also progresses the narrative through the use dialogue boxes, where characters interact with each other to reveal key plot points.

A brief playthrough showcasing gameplay mechanics and narrative

Mendax Boss Fight

When the player chooses to bring the chalice back to cure Ericus, Mendax appears as the final boss. Mendax possesses extreme amounts of health, yet has abysmally low speed. He summons a minion every second that cannot be targeted by the player's golems.

A playthough of the boss fight against Mendax

Lumenia Boss Fight

When the player chooses to drink from the chalice, Lumenia appears as the final boss. Lumenia possesses high speed and teleports to a random location on the map every two seconds. During her teleportation phase, she summons minions that cannot be targeted by the player's golems.

A playthough of the boss fight against Lumenia

Playtesting

What were the results?

These questions were created by the team to mainly analyze whether the narrative weaves in fluidly with the gameplay, and whether it motivates players to finish the story. The team also wanted to see whether players adopted unique strategies to tackle each wave.

01

Do our players feel as though there is a good balance of gameplay versus story?

The narration video did not exist during the first few iterations of the game. Instead, a screen full of text explaining the game's backstory was implemented. Players were dissuaded from reading it due to being visually jarring. Thus, players were unable to understand the game's storyline. Despite this, they appreciated the implementation of a dialogue system.

The team decided to implement a narration video complete with art and voice acting that establishes the lore of Eisos, the characters, and their motivations. All of the players sat through the video, claiming that it was "intriguing." As a result, players were able to understand the plot points being explained during dialogue interactions.

02

Can our players relate to the protagonist? Do they want to see her story unravel and does it motivate their gameplay?

Players claimed that the narration video clearly establishes Lucerna's motivations as well as her backstory, which enticed them to play through the game to find out more about her.

Some players even claimed to relate to the fact that Lucerna is shunned or feared by those close to her, which acted as a catalyst for wanting to complete the game. They felt as if they were playing virtual versions of themselves.

03

Did players create strategies to beat waves?

The team designed the golems in a way that each of them can either synergize well with other golems or the same types of golems. This was done through careful iterations of their traits such as cost, damage, and fire rate. Playtesting results depict that players created various strategies regarding golem placement on the map.

For example, players would place fire golems near the spawn points of enemies to deal damage to many of them, softening them up for lightning golems. Some players would even place ice golems near the chalice to slow enemies down while they used Lucerna's spells to whittle away at their health.

04

Did players find ways to counter the abilities of both Mendax and Lumenia?

The team wanted to emphasize mainly controlling Lucerna during the boss battles. This was done to add challenge and a way for players to feel as if Lucerna were taking matters into her own hands by defeating her parents. In terms of gameplay, this effect was achieved by making golems unable to target the minions summoned by the bosses, thus the player must use their own spells to eliminate them.

Overall, playtesting results show that players were able to develop strategies to counter the abilities of both Mendax and Lumenia. For Mendax, players favoured both lightning golems and fire golems to chip away and Mendax's health while they focused on eliminating the summoned minions. For Lumenia, players preffered to use ice golems to slow her down due to her immense speed.

Reflection

There were three major iterations of this project, with each one receiving critique from many playtesters, including our professor and TA.

In spite of this, the team faced numerous challenges, such as creating an alluring narrative and characters that players would care about, as well as weaving the narrative in seamlessly with the gameplay. The team also had to learn how to use Unity in such a short time frame.

From this experience, I learned the importance of building a solid relationship with team members, as well as plannning out goals and milestones to accomplish for each week. I am also thankful for being in a team composed of extremely hard-working individuals; without them, this project wouldn't have turned out the way it did.
