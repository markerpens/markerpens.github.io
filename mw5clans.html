Project Overview.

What is MechWarrior 5: Clans?

MechWarrior 5: Clans is a single-player, narrative-driven PVE BattleMech simulator. Players follow the story of Clan Smoke Jaguar and their endeavors to reclaim their homelands from the Inner Sphere tyrants. Players will experience this through the eyes of Jayden the protagonist, as he leads his squadron of 5 'Mechs across a multitude of planets to bring glory to the Smoke Jaguar name.

"We were made for this."

Responsibilities.

Mission Briefing Design / Documentation

Designed and implemented content for 37 mission briefing sequences. Comprehensively documented mission briefing design process/tools and reported to lead design producers to ensure that mission briefings met quality assurance standards.

Narrative Systems Implementation

Worked closely with lead narrative designers to implement, review, and ensure localization readiness for over 3000 lines of dialogue for mission briefing sequences, cinematics, and in-game levels for over 20 playable and non-playable characters.

POI Asset Planning

Helped technical artists and modellers to plan and create 15 unique Points of Interest 3D assets to populate the mission briefing maps and define spawn zones, enemy clusters, terrain types, and combat objectives.

Map Texture Implementation

Cooperated with engineering and art teams to generate 74 diffuse and heightmap textures optimize map visibility such as terrain types and heights, as well as maximizing POI visibility.

Audio / QA Assistance

Aided in implementing and bug-fixing audio files for all in-game content to ensure playback accuracy for QA. Reported any audio anomalies to the lead audio director.

Achievement Design

Designed 40 achievements, guiding players to explore the game's systems and mechanics (for instance, the SimPod, MechLab, etc). Coordinated with database leads to implement Steam achievements, Trophy rarities and Gamerscore values for the Playstation 5 and XBOX X|S consoles.

Design Challenges.

Problem

The main challenge that came up during the mission briefing designs process was the pacing. That is to say that a lot of briefings had large exposition, with nothing to visually aid the player through the dialogue (before the map textures and Point of Interest assets were shown). This resulted in a lot of skipping, which defeated the purpose of briefing the player of the mission objectives.

Solution

The solution was to incorporate more relevant images and assets to fill in these voids and tie them to the dialogue. Thanks to collaborations with the Technical Art and UX/UI teams, we were able to actualize this with implementing holographic images of assets such as planets, enemy NPCs, enemy factions, and adding zoom-in capabilities to map textures â€” resulting in more dynamic, well-paced mission briefings that bolsters the clarity of mission objectives and entices players to watch a briefing in its entirety.
